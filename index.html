Cyber Law & Intellectual Property

High-Yield Exam Topics — Unit-by-Unit Analysis

UNIT 1 Introduction to Cybercrimes

  • Classes and Categories of Cybercrime: Definitions and clear distinctions between different types.
  • Specific Targeted Crimes: Cybercrimes against women and children.
  • Financial Frauds: Understanding the mechanics of online financial crimes.
  • Social Engineering: Techniques and definitions of social engineering attacks.

UNIT 2 Cybercrime and Cyber Law

  • IT Act 2000: This is a critical legal framework; focus on its Amendments, various Offences, and legal perspectives.
  • Modern Attack Vectors: Definitions of Ransomware, Zero-day, and Zero-click attacks.
  • Cyber Security Organizations: Knowledge of Indian organizations dealing with cybercrime.

UNIT 3 Social Media Overview and Security

  • Privacy & Security Issues: Challenges and specific security risks related to online social networks.
  • Legal Aspects of Content: Laws regarding inappropriate content, as well as mechanisms for flagging and reporting.
  • Best Practices: Standard guidelines for safe social media use.

UNIT 4 Introduction to Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

  • Core Concepts of IPR: Detailed understanding of Patents, Copyrights, Trademarks, and Geographical Indications (GI).
  • International Frameworks: The role of WIPO, TRIPS, and WTO in regulating IPR.
  • Registration Processes: Practical aspects of registering various IPRs in India and abroad.

UNIT 5 Patents

  • Patent Classification: Difference between Product Patents and Process Patents.
  • Registration Lifecycle: Steps for patent search, registration, and granting.
  • Rights and Infringement: Exclusive rights of patent holders and what constitutes an infringement.
  • Legal Procedures: Concepts like compulsory licensing, revocation of patents, and the Patent Cooperation Treaty.

📋 Exam Strategy Summary (High-Yield Focus)

Focus Area Key Strategy
Definitions & Classifications Ensure you can define various cyber offences (Unit 1) and IPR types (Unit 4).
Legal Frameworks Memorize key sections of the IT Act 2000 and the roles of international bodies like WIPO.
Practical Procedures Be prepared to explain the step-by-step process for patent registration and reporting social media violations.
